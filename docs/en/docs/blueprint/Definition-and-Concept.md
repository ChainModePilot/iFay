## Product-specific name

### iFay
Individual Fay. A digital avatar attached to a natural person (referred to as the **Host**), which strives to ensure alignment with the natural person (Host) it is attached to in terms of values, personality, and habits.

### coFay
Common Fay, A digital avatar that assumes a specific role, roughly equivalent to the current concept of an Agent. It possesses general knowledge and skills in a certain field and can serve multiple people through conversations.

### Fay
A general term for anthropomorphic AI Agents. It may be an important node in new social relationships.

### FayGer
A container that provides a virtual runtime environment for Fay (iFay and coFay). This ensures that Fay can be attached to different hardware and software across platforms, enabling the hardware and software to possess the personal will of the Host.

### GMChain
**Global Merit Chain**, a blockchain where digital tokens are obtained by measuring recognizable social contribution. Unlike most blockchain projects, this project relies entirely on the recognition of social value to obtain tokens, rather than through completing predefined and undisputed computational tasks within the project. At the same time, in order to connect with the value recognition methods in the real world, it also accepts targeted issuance methods mortgaged by fiat currencies, bonds, asset certificates, gold, etc.

### MeriToken
Digital tokens, a type of quantitative digital credential corresponding to the degree of social contribution.

## Clarification of General Concepts

### Context
The external environment in which Fay exists includes communication partners, interaction patterns, messages conveyed, the meanings expressed by the messages, controllable hardware and software, callable resources and skills, etc.

### Protocol
Including the annotation definitions of data storage, message bodies, parameters, and interface structures, as well as the agreement on interaction processes for a specific purpose.

### Credentials
The credential here is a generalized concept, referring to any digital identifier used for the unique identification of people, events, or objects, which cannot be tampered with or denied. It includes unique IDs, account passwords, certificates, authorizations, access tokens, smart contracts, and digital tokens (we use MeriToken here).
