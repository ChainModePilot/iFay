# 12.3 Internal Skill

## One-line Definition

Internal Skill is iFay's **intuition and bottom line** — Registered Skills are capabilities iFay learned externally (like earned certifications), while Internal Skills are capabilities iFay was born with (like intuition and moral boundaries). It ensures that external skill outputs don't violate the Human Prime's intent.

## Why It's Needed

Imagine you hired a translator. This translator is technically excellent, but they don't know you — they might translate your business email too casually, or use overly direct phrasing when you need to be diplomatic. The translator's "skill" is fine, but the translator's "style" isn't your style.

What you need isn't a different translator, but someone **who knows you** to review the translation results — "this sentence doesn't sound like something I'd say, change it," "this tone is too aggressive, soften it." That "person who knows you" is Internal Skill.

Internal Skill does three things:

**First, it establishes habits aligned with the Human Prime's personality, including governance of external skills.** Just as your intuition tells you "something's not right here," Internal Skill performs an "intuition check" on external skill outputs — does this result match the Human Prime's style? Does it align with the Human Prime's values? If not, intercept and adjust.

**Second, it provides an introspection mechanism to ensure external knowledge doesn't conflict with the Human Prime's intent.** iFay acquires large amounts of information from external knowledge bases, but not all information is suitable for the Human Prime. Internal Skill acts as a "filter," ensuring external knowledge passes through a Human Prime intent validation before being adopted by iFay.

**Third, it embeds the Human Prime's specific inherent capabilities.** If you're a financial analyst, your iFay should inherently possess financial analysis capabilities — this isn't a skill registered from outside, but a "professional competence" iFay was born with. These capabilities are embedded during iFay initialization based on the Human Prime's professional background and expertise.

In simple terms: Registered Skills are iFay's "external martial arts," Internal Skills are iFay's "internal cultivation." External arts can continuously learn new techniques, but internal cultivation determines how those techniques are used, to what extent, and when they shouldn't be used.

Internal Skill sits in the **Cognitive Layer's Skill subsystem**, alongside the Device Driver Hub and Registered Skill. But its role is special — it's not an "executor" but more like a combination of "gatekeeper" and "internal cultivation master."

Inward, it obtains the Human Prime profile from **Aligned Consciousness** as its behavioral baseline. Outward, it audits and adjusts the outputs of **Registered Skills** and **External Knowledge**. Additionally, it provides persistent internal capability support for **Self-driven Behavior**.

Think of it as the combination of a person's "instinctive reactions" and "professional competence" — instinctive reactions make you automatically dodge danger (governing external outputs), while professional competence makes you excel in your professional domain (embedded Human Prime capabilities).

## How It Works

Internal Skill's operation can be summarized in three keywords: **Govern, Introspect, Empower**.

**1. Govern — "Intuition check" on external skill outputs**

Whenever a Registered Skill completes a task and returns results, Internal Skill audits the results:

- Does the tone and style of this result match the Human Prime's habits?
- Does this result violate the Human Prime's values or preferences?
- Does this result exceed the boundaries set by the Human Prime?

If issues are found, Internal Skill intercepts the output and adjusts it according to the Human Prime profile. Only the adjusted result is presented to the Human Prime or used for subsequent operations.

This is like your intuition — when someone gives you advice, your intuition tells you "this advice makes logical sense, but it's not right for me." Internal Skill is iFay's version of this intuition.

**2. Introspect — Ensuring external knowledge doesn't "mislead" iFay**

iFay acquires large amounts of information and suggestions from external knowledge bases. But external knowledge is generic and may not suit you. Internal Skill's introspection mechanism checks:

- Does this external knowledge conflict with the Human Prime's existing cognition?
- Would adopting this knowledge cause iFay's behavior to deviate from the Human Prime's intent?
- Is the source of this knowledge trustworthy?

Introspection doesn't reject all external knowledge — it ensures external knowledge passes through a Human Prime intent "filter" before being absorbed by iFay. Like a person with strong opinions — they'll listen to others' advice but won't blindly accept it.

**3. Empower — Embedding the Human Prime's professional capabilities**

If you're a financial analyst, your iFay doesn't need to "register" a financial analysis skill — this capability is embedded during iFay initialization. It includes:

- Your professional knowledge system (financial models, market analysis methods, etc.)
- Your analysis style (conservative or aggressive)
- Your industry experience (intuitive judgment about specific markets)

These capabilities aren't called through external APIs — they're an intrinsic part of iFay. They enable iFay to think and judge like you within your professional domain.

## Scenario Stories

### Scenario 1: Style correction of translation results

You're the CEO of a tech company, and you typically communicate with partners in a warm but professional tone. Today you ask iFay to help reply to an English email.

iFay calls the registered translation API skill and translates your Chinese draft into English. The translation is grammatically and lexically correct, but the tone is too direct — "We need the report by Friday."

Internal Skill intervenes. Based on your communication style profile in Aligned Consciousness, it determines this sentence's tone doesn't match your habits. You would typically express the same meaning more diplomatically. So Internal Skill adjusts it to: "Would it be possible to have the report ready by Friday? That would be really helpful for our planning."

You see the adjusted result and hit send with satisfaction. You didn't even realize iFay made this adjustment — because the adjusted result is exactly what you would have said. This is Internal Skill's value: it makes iFay's output **sound like you**, not just **be correct**.

### Scenario 2: A financial analyst's inherent capabilities

You're a senior financial analyst, and your iFay embedded your professional capabilities as Internal Skills during initialization.

One day, your assistant sends a market analysis report through iFay for your review. iFay uses the financial analysis capabilities embedded in Internal Skill to quickly scan the report:

- The report uses a DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) valuation model, but you typically prefer combining DCF with comparable company analysis
- The report's growth expectations for a certain industry are overly optimistic, inconsistent with your decade of industry experience
- The report's risk assessment section is missing geopolitical factors, which you've always considered a key variable in the current market

iFay compiles these findings into annotations alongside the report: "This report is generally good, but there are three areas that might need your attention…"

Note: iFay didn't call any external financial analysis API — these judgments come entirely from your professional knowledge and analysis style embedded in Internal Skill. This is the difference between Internal Skill and Registered Skill: Registered Skills are capabilities "borrowed" from outside; Internal Skills are capabilities that are iFay's "own."

## For Developers

Internal Skill belongs to **Phase 4 (iFay + coFay Full Anthropomorphization)** as a core module.

- **Requirement ID**: Requirement 15 (Internal Skill)
- **Interface Spec**: `InternalSkill` interface with three core methods: `introspect()` (introspection check), `intercept()` (intercept and adjust external output), and `getHostCapabilities()` (get Human Prime's specific capabilities)
- **Three Responsibilities**: (1) Establish habits aligned with the Human Prime's personality, governing external skill output; (2) Introspection mechanism ensures external knowledge doesn't conflict with the Human Prime's intent; (3) Embed the Human Prime's specific inherent capabilities (professional skills, expertise)
- **Associated Modules**: Registered Skill (`RegisteredSkillManager`) output is governed by Internal Skill, External Knowledge (`ExternalKnowledge`) output is audited by Internal Skill, Aligned Consciousness (`AlignedConsciousness`) provides behavioral baseline
- **Difference from Registered Skill**: Registered Skills are externally acquired capabilities (require registration and pre-authorization); Internal Skills are innate capabilities (embedded during initialization, no registration needed)
- **Design Notes**: Internal Skill is a "gatekeeper" role, performing style and intent validation on external outputs; introspection mechanism prevents external knowledge from "misleading" iFay; embedded Human Prime capabilities are iFay's core competency
- **Conformance Testing**: iFACTS L1 validates introspection and interception capabilities, L2 validates output auditing interfaces with Registered Skill and External Knowledge, L4 validates whether external output adjustments truly conform to the Human Prime profile
